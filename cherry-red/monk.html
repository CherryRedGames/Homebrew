Hit Dice

Hit Dice: 1d8 per monk level

Hit Points at 1st Level: 8 + your Constitution modifier

Hit Points at Higher Levels: 1d8 (or 5) + your Constitution modifier per monk level after 1st

Proficiencies

Armor: None

Weapons: Simple weapons, shortswords

Tools: Choose one type of artisan's tools or one musical instrument

Saving Throws: Strength, Dexterity

Skills: Choose 3 from Acrobatics, Athletics, History, Insight, Investigation, Medicine, Perception, Religion, Stealth

Equipment

You start with the following equipment in addition to the equipment granted by your background:

  • (a) a shortsword or (b) any simple weapon

  • (a) a dungeoneer's pack (b) an explorer's pack

1st Level: Martial Arts

Your practice in martial arts gives you mastery of combat styles that use unarmed strikes and monk weapons (which are all simple weapons and martial weapons without the Heavy or Two-Handed property).

You gain the following benefits while you are unarmed or wielding only monk weapons, provided that you’re not wearing any armor or wielding a shield:

  • You become proficient with improvised weapons and can use your Martial Arts die for damage rolls you make with improvised weapons. Improvised weapons are considered to be monk weapons for you.

  • You can use Dexterity instead of Strength for the attack and damage rolls of your unarmed strikes and monk weapons.

  • You can roll a d6 in place of the normal damage of your unarmed strikes. This die changes as you gain monk levels, as shown in the Martial Arts column of the monk table.

  • Whenever you make an Athletics check, you can choose to use your Dexterity modifier instead of Strength.

  • You can use your bonus action to make a single unarmed strike.

  • If you hit a creature with an unarmed strike, you can use your bonus action to attempt to Grapple them.

1st Level: Unarmored Defense

While you’re not wearing any armor or wielding a shield, your base AC is equal to 10 + your Dexterity and Wisdom modifier.

1st Level: Weapon Specialty

You gain the Weapon Specialty: Monk Weapons feat and become proficient in all weapons in the Monk group if you aren’t already. All weapons in the Monk group are considered to be monk weapons for you.

2nd Level: Flurry of Blows

Once per round, you can use your bonus action to make two unarmed strikes. You can use this feature a number of times per long rest equal to your Monk level. Whenever you finish a short rest, you regain expended uses of this feature equal to your Wisdom modififer (minimum 1).

2nd Level: Unarmored Movement

While not wearing armor or wielding a shield, you gain a bonus to your movement speed as shown in the Unarmored Movement column of the monk table.

Additionally, while not wearing armor or wielding a shield, your movement does not trigger attacks of opportunity and you can take the dash or dodge action as a bonus action.

3rd Level: Heightened Reaction

Your training has enabled you to react quickly, granting you the following benefits:

  • Deflect Attack. You can use your reaction to deflect or catch a strike when you’re hit with an attack that would deal bludgeoning, piercing, or slashing damage. When you do, roll your martial arts die- reduce the damage you would have taken by that amount. If you reduce the bludgeoning, slashing or piercing damage to 0, you can make one unarmed strike against the creature if it’s in range as part of the same reaction. If the attack was a ranged attack, and you reduced the damage to 0, you can throw the missile back if the missile is small enough to hold in one hand and you have at least one free hand. The attack made this way uses your proficiency bonus and Dexterity modifier for the attack and damage roll, and its range becomes 60 ft.

  • Emboldened Initiative. Whenever you roll initiative, you can add your Wisdom modifier to the roll.

3rd Level: Monk Subclass

You gain one monk subclass of your choice. You gain each of your subclass’s features that are of your monk level and lower. This class’s description tells you the levels when your subclass provides features.

4th Level: Feat

You gain the Ability Score Improvement feat or another feat of your choice for which you qualify

4th Level: Slow Fall

Any fall damage that you would take is reduced by an amount equal to five times your monk level.

5th Level: Extra Attack

You can attack twice, instead of once, whenever you take the attack action on your turn.

5th Level: Stunning Strike

Once per turn, when you hit another creature with a melee weapon attack or unarmed strike, you can attempt to give the creature the Stunned condition. The creature must make a Constitution saving throw. The DC is 8 + your proficiency bonus + your Wisdom modifier (minimum 1). On a failed saving throw, the creature is Stunned until the end of your next turn. On a successful saving throw, the creature is instead Dazed (can use their movement or action, not both and cannot take bonus actions or reactions) until the end of your next turn. You cannot use this feature on a creature that is already Stunned or Dazed from this feature.

You can use this feature a number of times per short or long rest equal to your monk level.

6th Level: Accelerated Reaction

You now have two reactions per round, instead of one.

6th Level: Subclass Feature

You gain a feature from your monk subclass.

7th Level: Evasion

When you are subjected to an effect that allows you to make a Dexterity saving throw to only take half damage, you instead take no damage if you succeed and only half damage if you fail.

7th Level: Heightened Metabolism

Once per long rest, if you spend at least one minute resting, you can gain the benefits of a short rest.

8th Level: Feat

You gain the Ability Score Improvement feat or another feat of your choice for which you qualify.

8th Level: Spiritual Guidance

You learn the Guidance cantrip. Additionally, you can cast Enhance Ability at its base level as an action without using a spell slot or material components a number of times per long rest equal to your Wisdom modifier (minimum 1).

9th Level: Acrobatic Movement

While not wearing armor or wielding a shield, you gain the ability to move along vertical surfaces and across liquids on your turn without falling during the movement.

9th Level: Purity of Body and Mind

You cannot be Charmed, Frightened, Paralyzed, Poisoned, or Stunned and you become immune to Poison and Psychic damage. Additionally, forgoing food or drink doesn’t give you levels of exhaustion.

10th Level: Feat

You gain the Ability Score Improvement feat or another feat of your choice for which you qualify.

10th Level: Subclass Feature

You gain a feature from your monk subclass.

11th Level: Deflect Energy

You can use the Deflect Attack option of your Heightened Reaction feature against ranged attacks that deal any type of damage.

12th Level: Feat

You gain the Ability Score Improvement feat or another feat of your choice for which you qualify.

13th Level: Disciplined Soul

You become proficient in all saving throws. Additionally, a number of times per short or long rest equal to your Wisdom modifier (minimum 1), whenever you make a saving throw and fail, you can choose to reroll it, but you must take the new result.

14th Level: Subclass Feature

You gain a feature from your monk subclass.

16th Level: Feat

You gain the Ability Score Improvement feat or another feat of your choice for which you qualify.

18th Level: Superior Defense

As a bonus action, you can become resistant to all damage types except for force damage for the next minute. If you are Incapacitated within the minute, this effect ends early. You can use this feature twice per long rest.

19th Level: Feat

You gain the Ability Score Improvement feat or another feat of your choice for which you qualify.

20th Level: Enlightenment

You gain the following benefits:

  • Your Dexterity and Wisdom scores increase by 2 and their maximums are now 22.

  • You gain 30 ft. of truesight.

  • Once per long rest, you can cast Foresight without expending a spell slot or needing material components. However, the duration of the spell becomes 4 hours.

Way of the Auric

Through your training, you’ve learned to harness aura- a form of life energy that radiates around and within the living. Those that can see the aura of others can discern their emotions based on the color of their aura.

3rd Level: Auric Vision

You can see the colors of the auras of living creatures within 30 ft. of you- even if they’re hidden or Invisible. The color of their aura varies based on their mood. You have advantage on Insight checks against a creature if you can see the color of their aura. As long as you can see a creature’s aura, they cannot gain any benefits from being Invisible to you. This feature does not work on constructs or the undead.

3rd Level: Mood Boost

As a bonus action, you can channel your emotions to empower yourself or a creature within 15 ft. of you. The effect that you give depends on the color of aura you choose to radiate.

  • Red. Their damage rolls are increased by 2 until the start of your next turn.

  • Orange. They have advantage on saving throws against being frightened until the start of your next turn.

  • Yellow. Their movement speed increases by 10 ft. until the start of your next turn.

  • Green. They recover 2d4 hit points.

  • Blue. They gain a d6 inspiration die that lasts for the next minute.

  • Indigo. They have advantage on saving throws against being Charmed until the start of your next turn.

  • Violet. Their Intelligence and Charisma checks are increased by 2 until the start of your next turn.

  • Magenta. All damage the creature takes is reduced by your Wisdom modifier (minimum 1) until the start of your next turn.

  • White. All of their rolls are increased by 1 until the start of your next turn.

  • Black. Their attack rolls are increased by 2 until the start of your next turn.

You can use this feature a number of times per long rest equal to your Wisdom modifier (minimum 1). You regain one expended use of this feature whenever you finish a short rest.

6th Level: Auric Blast

You learn how to manifest your aura to blast your foes. As an action, you can make two ranged spell attacks- each with a range of 60 ft. You are proficient with it and you add your Wisdom modifier to its attack and damage rolls. On a hit, a creature suffers force damage equal to two rolls of your Martial Arts die.

10th Level: Empowered Mood

The range of your Mood Boost feature doubles to 30 ft. and the benefits of your Mood Boost feature double. For example, a creature gains a d12 inspiration die if you choose blue and a creature’s damage is reduced by double your Wisdom modifier if you choose magenta. If you choose orange or indigo, and the creature is currently Frightened (orange) or Charmed (indigo), you can end the condition.

10th Level: Surging Aura

Whenever you roll initiative, you gain a number of temporary hit points equal to your monk level. These temporary hit points last 1 minute.

14th Level: Forced Empathy

You learn to alter a creature’s life energy to mimic the injuries of others. Once per short or long rest, as a reaction, when a creature within 30 ft. of you takes damage, you can use your reaction to choose another creature within 30 ft. of you feel the same pain that they did. The chosen creature must make a Constitution saving throw. The DC is 8 + your proficiency bonus + your Wisdom modifier. If the creature fails, they lose the same amount of hit points as the other creature did. On a successful saving throw, the creature only loses half the hit points that the other creature lost.

Way of the Blood Knuckle

These monks use the tactics that they’ve learned to brutalize their enemies. Unlike other monks, they do not align themselves to a monastery or other affiliation. Instead, many of these were monks in training that abandoned their mentors in order to pursue their personal goals.

3rd Level: Bleeding Strikes

A number of times per short or long rest equal to twice your proficiency bonus (minimum 1), whenever you land an attack using an unarmed strike or monk weapon, you can choose to cause the creature you hit to bleed severely. When you do, roll your martials arts die once- that creature loses hit points equal to the number you roll. At the start of that creature’s turn, while they are bleeding this way, roll your martial arts die again- that creature loses hit points equal to the number you roll. A creature will bleed for up to 1 minute or until a creature uses an action to stop the bleeding or the creature recovers hit points from a magical source. You cannot use this feature on a creature that is already affected by this feature.

Some creatures may be unaffected by this feature (such as animated armor, a ghost, a skeleton, or any other creature chosen by the DM).

3rd Level: Improved Critical

You land a critical hit on a 19 as well as a 20 when using your unarmed strike or a monk weapon.

6th Level: Brutal Brawler

You can add your Strength modifier (minimum 0) to the attack roll and damage rolls of your unarmed strikes and monk weapons in addition to your Dexterity modifier. Additionally, whenever you land an attack on a creature that is bleeding from your Bleeding Strikes feature, you deal additional damage of the same type, equal to your proficiency bonus.

6th Level: Hardened Body

Your body has become hardened from your constant brawling. Your hitpoint maximum increases by an amount equal to twice your monk level. Whenever you gain a monk level hereafter, your hit point maximum increases by an additional 2 hit points. Additionally, you gain resistance to bludgeoning damage.

10th Level: Concussing Strike

Your strikes are so potent that you can concuss your enemies with a single blow. A number of times per short or long rest equal to your Wisdom modifier (minimum 1), when you hit a creature with an unarmed strike or monk weapon, you can have that creature make an Intelligence saving throw. The DC is 8 + your proficiency bonus + Wisdom modifier. On a failed saving throw, the creature takes psychic damage equal to half your monk level (rounded down) and is under the effects of the Confusion spell for the next minute (they can repeat the saving throw at the end of each of their turns to end the effect early). On a successful saving throw, the creature only takes half of the psychic damage.

14th Level: Brutal Critical

Whenever you land a critical hit with an unarmed strike or monk weapon, you can add half your monk level (rounded down) to the damage. This additional damage is the same type as your attack and is not doubled.

Way of the Desert Spirit

The desert can be a harsh environment: intense heat, no water, and vast expanses of sand. These monks train their bodies and minds against the environment.

3rd Level: Desert Attunement

Your training within the desert has allowed you to attune to it. A number of times per short or long rest equal to your Wisdom modifier (minimum 1), you can cast a spell on the following table at its base level without expending a spell slot or material components. However, the spell’s base level must be less than or equal to your proficiency bonus. When you cast a spell using this feature, you use the spell’s casting time. However, if you cast Dust Devil using this feature, you can choose to cast it as a bonus action. The saving throw for these spells is 8 + your proficiency bonus + your Wisdom modifier.

6th Level: Quicksand Pit

As a bonus action, you can cause the ground underneath a Large-sized or smaller creature to turn into quicksand for up to 1 minute. That creature must make a Strength saving throw (DC 8 + your proficiency bonus + your Wisdom modifier). On a failed saving throw, the creature is considered to be Grappled. A Grappled creature can attempt to use their action to break out- repeating the saving throw. On a successful saving throw, the creature’s speed is halved. However, a creature Grappled by the quicksand that fails the saving throw sinks deeper into the quicksand and becomes Restrained. A creature entering the quicksand area on their turn or ending their turn within the pit must make the initial saving throw. You can use this feature a number of times per short or long rest equal to your Wisdom modifier (minimum 1).

6th Level: Traveler's Excursions

Your body becomes more resilient to harsher conditions due to your travels. As such, you gain the following effects:

  • Your Constitution score increases by 2, to a maximum of 20.

  • Whenever you take a short rest, you can reduce your exhaustion by one level.

  • Whenever you take a long rest, you can reduce your exhaustion by two levels.

10th Level: Conjure Desert Spirits

As an action, you can conjure a number of desert spirits equal to half your proficiency bonus. These spirits move immedietely after you in initiative. You can choose the appearance of these spirits, but they use the Dust Mephit stat block with the following changes:

  • The Dust Mephits have 10 + your monk level in hit points.

  • If a Dust Mephit makes an attack with its claws, it uses the same bonus you get to making an unarmed attack.

  • The DC of any saving throw the Dust Mephit causes is equal to 8 + your proficiency bonus + your Wisdom modifier (minimum 1).

  • If you would be in range of the Dust Mephit's Death Burst or Blinding Breath effects, you automatically succeed the saving throw.

You can only use this feature once per long rest. When you finish a long rest, any spirits that you summoned this way vanish.

10th Level: Desert's Resilience

Your body is adept at withstanding the harsh desert conditions. You gain resistance to cold and fire damage. Additionally, natural, environmental effects such as intense cold and heat have no negative effect on you.

14th Level: Sandstorm Surge

A number of times per short or long rest equal to your Wisdom modifier (minimum 1), you can create a sandstorm to barrage your foes. As an action, you cause a small sandstorm in a 20 ft. radius sphere centered on a point that you can see within 90 ft. of you. All creatures that start their turn within the radius or enter the radius must make a Constitution saving throw (DC 8 + your proficiency bonus + your Wisdom modifier). On a failed saving throw, the creature suffers 6d8 slashing damage. On a successful saving throw, they take half the damage. Creatures other than you cannot see through the sandstorm and are Blinded while within its range. The sandstorm lasts up to 10 minutes and you can move it up to 30 ft. as a bonus action. The sandstorm ends early if you are Incapacitated.

Way of the Drunken Master (Rework)

3rd Level: Bonus Proficiencies

You become proficient in the Deception and Performance skills if you aren’t already. You also gain proficiency with brewer’s supplies and improvised weapons.

3rd Level: Drunken Strike

You learn how to effectively strike at your foes- channeling your drunken techniques through your strikes to discombobulate them. Once per turn, when you hit a creature with an attack from a monk weapon or an unarmed strike, you can impose disadvantage on the next attack roll that the creature makes before the end of their next turn.

3rd Level: Unpredictable Strike

Your first attack with your Flurry of Blows is made at advantage.

6rd Level: Antagonizing Act

Your erratic movements and foolhardy facade embarrasses and aggravates your enemies all at once. A number of times per short or long rest equal to your Wisdom modifier (minimum 1), when you land a critical hit against a creature with an unarmed strike or monk weapon attack OR a creature misses you with a melee attack roll, you can cast Antagonize at its base level on the creature without expending a spell slot or using any verbal, somatic, or material components. The spell save DC is 8 + your proficiency bonus + your Wisdom modifier (minimum 1). If you or your allies are the only creatures that the target can attack with their reaction from this spell, you can choose for the creature’s next attack to be at disadvantage instead of attacking.

6th Level: Tipsy Sway

You drunken movements grant you the following benefits:

  • Drunken Stance. Attack rolls cannot have advantage against you unless you are Incapacitated, Paralyzed, Petrified, Restrained, or Stunned.

  • Leap to Your Feet. When you’re Prone, you can stand up by only using 5 ft. of movement instead of half your movement speed. Once per turn, when you get up from being Prone, you can make one unarmed strike as part of your movement.

  • Redirect Attack. When a creature misses you with a melee attack, you can cause the attack to target one other creature of your choice, other than the attacker, that you can see within the attack’s range. This redirected attack uses the initial attack roll.

  • Sudden Strike. Whenever a creature enters the melee range of your unarmed strikes or monk weapon, you can make an unarmed strike as a reaction.

10th Level: Drunkard's Luck

You always seem to get a lucky bounce at the right moment. You gain the Lucky feat and can regain luck points from this feature whenever you finish a short or long rest. If you already have the Lucky feat, the number of luck points you have becomes equal to your Wisdom modifier (minimum 4).

10th Level: Improved Drunken Strike

You can now use your Drunken Strike feature twice per turn. However, you must use it on two separate creatures.

14th Level: Erratic Strikes

All unarmed strikes that you make from your Flurry of Blows feature are made at advantage.

Way of Fire Dancer

Monks of this tradition are trained how to perform dances with flaming weapons. This technique is to teach them the importance of discipline and honoring tradition. The heat from the flames toughens their bodies and the dangers of fire teach them the importance of practice and responsibility.

3rd Level: Flaming Weaponry

As a bonus action, you can alight a monk weapon with fire. For the next minute, the weapon deals additional fire damage equal to your Wisdom modifier (minimum 1).

3rd Level: Dancer's Boon

Your training grants you the following benefits:

  • Your Charisma score increases by 2, to a maximum of 20.

  • You gain proficiency in the Performance skill if you don’t already have it. If you’re already proficient in it, you gain expertise in it.

6th Level: Flaming Performer

Your training grants you a resistance to fire damage. Additionally, any unarmed strikes that you make deal additional fire damage equal to your Wisdom modifier (minimum 1).

10th Level: Entrancing Display

Your performances with your weapons become entrancing. While wielding a weapon from your Additional Weapon Proficiencies feature that is on fire, you can use an action to cast Enthrall or Hypnotic Pattern at their base levels without using a spell slot, material components, or verbal components. You can use this feature a number of times per long rest equal to your Wisdom modifier (minimum 1).

14th Level: Dance of Flame

As an action, you can perform a complex, fiery dance to inspire your allies. If you do, choose one creature (other than yourself) that can see you within 30 ft. of you: they can immediately take an action without using their reaction. When you perform this dance, you gain the effects of the Fire Shield spell for the next 10 minutes. You can perform this dance a number of times per long rest equal to half your proficiency bonus (rounded down).

Way of the Shadowboxer

Shadowboxing is a training technique where someone boxes with an imaginary opponent. These monks have become adept with this method of practice and have incorporated it into their battles- even manifesting shadows of themselves to fight alongside them.

3rd Level: Elusive Shadow

You learn how to get the slip on your enemies and retaliate. Once per round, immediately after a creature makes an attack roll against you, you can move up to half your movement speed.

3rd Level: Shadowboxing Drills

Your shadowboxing technique allows you to fight better. Whenever you finish a short or long rest, you can roll a d20 a number of times equal to half your proficiency bonus (rounded down) and record the numbers. Whenever you make a weapon attack or unarmed strike, or an enemy makes an attack roll against you, you can change the roll for one of the recorded numbers. Once a number is used it disappears. When you begin a short or long rest and still have unused numbers, the numbers disappear.

6th Level: Out-Boxer

Your practice allows you to hit your foes from a distance and keep away. Whenever you make a melee attack with a melee weapon in the monk group or make an unarmed strike, you can increase your attack’s reach by 5 ft.

6th Level: Shadow Decoy

A number of times per short or long rest equal to your Wisdom modifier, you can create a decoy of yourself to either protect you from an attack or overwhelm your enemy. As a bonus action OR as a reaction to when you are hit by an attack, you can choose to take half of the attack’s damage and teleport up to 30 ft. away to an unoccupied space that you can see- summoning a shadowboxer in the space you just left.

The Shadowboxer resembles yourself, but is made out of shadow. The Shadowboxer has the same stats, monk features, AC, and movement speed as you. If your shadowboxer uses a monk feature, it uses one of your uses for it. Its hit points are equal to twice your Monk level. During your turn, the Shadowboxer can act and move as you wish. The Shadowboxer disappears at the end of your next turn or if its hit points are reduced to 0.

10th Level: Muscle Memory

Each time you land an attack roll against a creature, all attack rolls that you make against that creature are increased by 1 (up to your proficiency bonus) until the start of your next turn.

14th Level: Shadowboxing Flurry

Your Flurry of Blows feature grants you three unarmed strikes instead of two.
