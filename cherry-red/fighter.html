Hit Dice

Hit Dice: 1d10 per fighter level

Hit Points at 1st Level: 10 + your Constitution modifier

Hit Points at Higher Levels: 1d10 (or 6) + your Constitution modifier per fighter level after 1st

Proficiencies

Armor: All armor, shields

Weapons: Simple weapons, martial weapons

Tools: None

Saving Throws: Strength, Constitution

Skills: Choose 2 from Acrobatics, Athletics, History, Insight, Intimidation, Perception, Survival

Equipment

You start with the following equipment in addition to the equipment granted by your background:

  • (a) chain mail or (b) leather armor

  • (a) a martial melee weapon and shield or (b) a longbow and 20 arrows

  • (a) two simple weapons or (b) one martial weapon

  • (a) a dungeoneer's pack or (b) an explorer's pack

1st Level: Fighting Style

You gain one Fighting Style feat of your choice.

1st Level: Second wind

As a bonus action, you can expend a use of your Second Wind to regain hit points equal to 1d10 + your fighter level. You can use this feature a number of times per long rest as shown in the Second Wind column of the fighter table. Whenever you finish a short rest, you regain one expended use of this feature.

1st Level: Weapon Specialty

You gain one Weapon Specialty feat of your choice.

2nd Level: Action Surge

On your turn, you can take one additional action, as long as it is not casting a spell or using a magical item. Once you use this feature, you must finish a short or long rest before you can use it again.

2nd Level: Combat Superiority

Maneuvers. You learn three battle maneuvers of your choice. You can only use one maneuver per attack. You learn additional maneuvers of your choice as shown in the Maneuvers Known column of the fighter table. Whenever you take a short or long rest, you can replace one of the maneuvers you know with a different one.

Superiority Dice. You have a number of superiority dice, which are d8s, as shown in the Superiority Dice column of the fighter table. A superiority die is expended when you use it. You regain all expended superiority dice when you finish a short or long rest.

Second Wind. You regain one expended superiority die whenever you use your Second Wind feature.

Saving Throws. Some of your maneuvers require your target to make a saving throw. The saving throw DC is 8 + your proficiency bonus + your Strength or Dexterity modifier (your choice).

3rd Level: Fighter Subclass

You gain one fighter subclass of your choice. You gain each of your subclass’s features that are of your fighter level and lower. This class’s description tells you the levels when your subclass provides features.

4th Level: Feat

You gain the Ability Score Improvement feat or another feat of your choice for which you qualify.

5th Level: Extra Attack

You can attack twice, instead of once, whenever you take the attack action on your turn

5th Level: Tactical Shift

When you expend a use of your Second Wind as a bonus action, you can move up to half of your movement speed without provoking opportunity attacks.

6th Level: Feat

You gain the Ability Score Improvement feat or another feat of your choice for which you qualify.

6th Level: Subclass Feature

You gain a feature from your fighter subclass.

7th Level: Weapon Specialty

You gain one Weapon Specialty feat of your choice.

8th Level: Feat

You gain the Ability Score Improvement feat or another feat of your choice for which you qualify.

9th Level: Indomitable

If you fail a saving throw, you can reroll it with a bonus equal to your fighter level. If you do so, you must use the new roll. You can use this feature a number of times per long rest equal to half your proficiency bonus (rounded down).

10th Level: Feat

You gain the Ability Score Improvement feat or another feat of your choice for which you qualify.

10th Level: Subclass Feature

You gain a feature from your fighter subclass.

11th Level: Two Extra Attacks

Your Extra Attack feature now confers two extra attacks instead of one

12th Level: Feat

You gain the Ability Score Improvement feat or another feat of your choice for which you qualify.

13th Level: Studied Attacks

You’ve learned how to read how your foes move in battle. If you make an attack roll against a creature and miss, you have advantage on your next attack roll against that creature until the end of your next turn.

13th Level: Unconquerable

If you fail a saving throw and your Indomitable feature is expended, you can use it again by expending a use of your Second Wind feature, which also heals you and restores a superiority die.

14th Level: Subclass Feature

You gain a feature from your fighter subclass.

15th Level: Improved Action Surge

You can use your Action Surge feature twice per short or long rest, instead of once. However, you can only use it once per turn.

16th Level: Feat

You gain the Ability Score Improvement feat or another feat of your choice for which you qualify.

17th Level: Warrior's Resolve

Once per long rest, when you would be reduced to 0 hit points and not be outright killed, you instead fall to 1 hit point.

18th Level: Three Extra Attacks

Your Extra Attack feature now confers three extra attacks instead of two

19th Level: Feat

You gain the Ability Score Improvement feat or another feat of your choice for which you qualify.

20th Level: Extraordinary Action

Your physical prowess enables you to perform fantastical physical feats. Depending on the weapon you’re wielding, you can do the following:

  • Melee Weapon. As an action, you can cast Steel Wind Strike at its base level without using a spell slot or any material components.

  • Ranged Weapon. As an action, you can cast Conjure Volley at its base level without using a spell slot or any material components.

You can use this feature three times per long rest.

Forge Warrior

These warriors have a history with the forge. Some have worked at smithing guilds while others try to create their own weapons to hone their craft. These fighters have a passion for their gear, valuing them higher than most other possessions. Many choose to fight with the tools they’ve made as a way to show off their work to others or find a place to work. No matter the fighter, the forge holds a place in their heart.

3rd Level: Favorite Weapon

Your love for specific weapons grants you insights that boost your fighting abilities. Choose one type of simple melee weapon or one type of martial melee weapon: you can add your proficiency bonus to any damage rolls you make using that weapon.

3rd Level: Forge Warrior

Your work in the forge has honed your capabilities. As such, you become proficient in Smith’s Tools. Additionally, you learn the Mending cantrip.

6th Level: Trade Secrets

You know how to create certain types of armor and weaponry. To make these items, you need to gather resources that have a cost equal to half of the item’s normal cost and you need access to a forge or similar place to work. To you, a workday is 8 hours.

The items you can make must be made with a significant amount of metal. For example, even though a 10th level fighter can make medium armor, they cannot make Hide Armor as it is primarily made of furs. They can still make shields as metal is a significant component.

Fighter Level Item Type Time to Make
6th Simple Melee Wapons 1 workday
6th Shields 2 workdays
10th Martial Melee Weapons 3 workdays
10th Medium Armor 5 workdays
14th Heavy Armor 7 workdays

The secrets you’ve learned from your work also allow you to make the gear you’ve made better. Any shields or armor that you make grants an additional +1 AC to its wearer- as long as they’re proficient in the armor or shield. Additionally, any weapon that you make deals an additional 2 points of damage of the same damage type- as long as its wielder is proficient in the weapon. Despite these benefits, the gear that you create is not considered to be magical.

10th Level: Journeyman

Your travels have given you a variety of new skills. You can choose two more skills to be proficient in. You’ve also become somewhat renowned with your gear- allowing you to easily find a place to work in any town.

Your travels also grant you an additional Fighting Style feat of your choice.

14th Level: Forged by the Flame

Your body has adapted to the harsh environment that you work in. You become resistant to fire damage. Additionally, your body’s become toughened from the occasional mishap. As such, you become resistant to one of the following damage types of your choice: bludgeoning, piercing, or slashing.

Heretic

Heretics are those whose core opinions and beliefs run counter to others. It’s their steadfast commitment to these beliefs that often spur persecution and brand them with the title of heretic. In battle, they draw the ire of their foes. Think about what your character's morals and ideals are. What do they believe in and how does it go against the popular consensus?

3rd Level: Beguiling Tongue

You gain the following benefits:

  • Your Charisma score increases by 2, to a maximum of 20.

  • You become proficient in one of the following skills of your choice: Decpetion, Intimidation, or Persuasion.

  • You learn the Vicious Mockery cantrip and can cast it as a bonus action a number of times per short or long rest equal to your Charisma modifier (minimum 1).

  • The spell save DC for any spells or feats that this subclass grants you is equal to 8 + your proficiency bonus + your Charisma modifier.

3rd Level: Scathing Taunt

The heretic naturally draws the ire of others, and you can use that to your advantage. As a bonus action, you can have all creatures of your choice within 30 ft. of you that can hear you make a Wisdom saving throw against your spell save DC. On a failed saving throw, the creature’s attack rolls against all creatures other than you are reduced by your Charisma modifier (minimum 1) while they can see you for the next minute. Additionally, you can add your Charisma modifier to any attack rolls and damage rolls that you make against a creature that failed this saving throw for the next minute. This effect ends early if you become Incapacitated. A creature that succeeds the saving throw is immune to this feature for 24 fours. You can use this feature a number of times per long rest equal to your Charisma modifier (minimum 1).

6th Level: Heretic's Cunning

You’ve learned how to manipulate the anger of your foes to your advantage in battle. A number of times per short or long rest equal to your Charisma modifier (minimum 1), you can cast a spell on the following table at its base level without expending a spell slot or material components. When you cast a spell using this feature, you can do so as a bonus action, instead of the spell’s casting time.

Spell Level Spells
1 Compelled Duel, Tasha's Hideous Laughter
2 Crown of Madness, Enthrall

6th Level: Surge of Defiance

When you roll initiative, you gain 1d6 temporary hit points for every enemy that you can see that is within 60 ft. of you, up to your proficiency bonus. For example, if you see four enemies within 60 ft. of you, but your proficiency bonus is +3, you only gain 3d6 temporary hit points. These temporary hit points last for up to 1 minute.

10th Level: Defiant Willpower

You have advantage on saving throws against being Charmed.

10th Level: Retaliation

In the heat of battle, you’re a master at retaliating against your foes. When a creature lands an attack roll against you while in range of one of your weapons, you can use your reaction to make an attack roll against that creature. If the attack that triggers this reaction was a critical hit, your attack roll is made with advantage.

14th Level: Undying Defiance

Your willpower enables you to withstand even the deadliest of strikes. Once per long rest, when your hit points would be reduced to 0 and you are not outright killed, you can instead remain at 1 hit point.

Additionally, you become proficient in Charisma saving throws.

New & Modified Battle Maneuvers

Blinding Gouge

When you hit a creature with a weapon attack, you can expend a superiority die to strike at their eyes. You can add what you rolled on your superiority die to the damage roll. The creature must make a Constitution saving throw. On a failed saving throw, the creature is Blinded until the end of their next turn.

Parry

When another creature would hit you with a melee attack, and you are wielding a melee weapon, you can expend one superiority die to increase your AC by a roll of your superiority die against the triggering attack only- possibly turning the hit to a miss. If the attack still hits you, you can reduce the damage by the number you rolled on the superiority die.

Sweeping Attack

When you hit a creature with a melee weapon attack, you can expend one superiority die to attempt to damage another creature with the same attack. Choose another creature within your weapon’s reach. If the original attack roll would have hit the second creature, it is also hit with the attack. You can add the superiority die to the attack’s damage roll.
