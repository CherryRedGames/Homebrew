Hit Dice

Hit Dice: 1d8 per rogue level

Hit Points at 1st Level: 8 + your Constitution modifier

Hit Points at Higher Levels: 1d8 (or 5) + your Constitution modifier per rogue level after 1st

Proficiencies

Armor: Light armor

Weapons: Simple weapons, hand crossbows, longswords, rapiers, shortswords

Tools: Thieves' tools

Saving Throws: Dexterity, Intelligence

Skills: Choose 4 from Acrobatics, Athletics, Deception, Insight, Intimidation, Investigation, Perception, Persuasion, Sleight of Hand, Stealth

Equipment

You start with the following equipment in addition to the equipment granted by your background:

  • (a) a rapier or (b) a shortsword

  • Leather armor, 2 daggers, and thieves' tools

  • (a) a handcrossbow and 20 bolts or (b) a shortbow and 20 arrows

  • (a) a burglar's pack, (b) a dungeoneer's pack, or (c) an explorer's pack

1st Level: Expertise

You gain expertise in two skill proficiencies of your choice.

1st Level: Sneak Attack

Once per turn, you can deal extra damage to one creature you hit with an attack roll if you’re attacking with a finesse or ranged weapon and you meet one of the following requirements:

  • You have advantage on the attack roll.

  • At least one of your allies is within 5 ft. of the target and isn’t Incapacitated.

1st Level: Weapon Specialty

You gain the Weapon Specialty: Rogue Weapons feat and become proficient in all weapons in the Rogue group if you aren’t already.

2nd Level: Cunning Action

As a bonus action, you can take the dash, disengage, or hide action.

2nd Level: Thieves' Cant

You learn Thieves’ Cant and one other language of your choice.

3rd Level: Steady Aim

If you haven’t moved on your turn, you can use your bonus action to give yourself advantage on the next attack roll you make this turn. When you use this feature, your movement speed is reduced to 0 until the end of your turn.

3rd Level: Subclass Feature

You gain a feature from your rogue subclass.

4th Level: Feat

You gain the Ability Score Improvement feat or another feat of your choice for which you qualify.

5th Level: Cunning Strike

You have developed cunning ways to use your Sneak Attack. When you deal Sneak Attack damage, you can add one of the following Cunning Strike effects. Each effect has a die cost, which is the number of Sneak Attack damage dice which you must forego to add the effect. You remove the die before rolling and the effect occurs immediately after the attack’s damage is dealt.

If a Cunning Strike effect requires a saving throw, the DC equals 8 + your Proficiency Bonus + your Dexterity modifier.

  • Disarm (Cost: 1d8). The target must succeed on a Dexterity saving throw or it drops one item of your choice that it’s holding.

  • Poison (Cost 1d8). You add a toxin to your strike, forcing the target to make a Constitution saving throw. On a failed save, the target has the Poisoned condition for 1 minute. At the end of each of its turns, it can repeat the saving throw, ending the effects on itself on a success. To use this effect, you must have a Poisoner's Kit on your person.

  • Trip (Cost: 1d8). If the target is Large or smaller, it must succeed on a Dexterity saving throw or have the Prone condition.

  • Withdraw (Cost: 1d8). Immediately after the attack, you move up to half your movement speed without provoking opportunity attacks or using your movement.

5th Level: Uncanny Dodge

When an attacker that you can see hits you with an attack roll, you can use your reaction to halve the attack’s damage against you (rounded down).

6th Level: Subclass Feature

You gain a feature from your rogue subclass.

7th Level: Evasion

Whenever you’re subjected to an effect that allows you to make a Dexterity saving throw to take half damage, you instead take no damage if you succeed and half damage if you fail. You can’t use this feature if you’re Incapacitated.

7th Level: Reliable Talent

Whenever you make an ability check that uses one of your skill or tool proficiencies, you can treat a d20 roll of a 9 or lower as a 10.

8th Level: Feat

You gain the Ability Score Improvement feat or another feat of your choice for which you qualify.

9th Level: Expertise

You gain expertise in two skill proficiencies of your choice.

10th Level: Feat

You gain the Ability Score Improvement feat or another feat of your choice for which you qualify.

10th Level: Subclass Feature

You gain a feature from your rogue subclass.

11th Level: Improved Cunning Strike

You can now use up to two Cunning Strike effects when you deal Sneak Attack damage, paying the die cost for each effect.

12th Level: Feat

You gain the Ability Score Improvement feat or another feat of your choice for which you qualify.

13th Level: Devious Strikes

You have practiced new ways to use your Sneak Attack deviously. The following effects are now among your Cunning Strike options:

  • Daze (Cost: 2d8). The target must succeed a Constitution saving throw, or it has the Dazed condition until the end of its next turn.

  • Knock Out (Cost: 4d8). The target must succeed on a Constitution saving throw, or it has the Unconscious condition for 1 minute or until it takes any damage. The Unconscious target can repeat the save at the end of each of its turns, ending the effect on itself on a success.

  • Obscure (Cost: 3d8). You strike near the target’s eyes. The target must succeed on a Dexterity saving throw, or it has the Blinded condition until the end of its next turn.

14th Level: Subclass Feature

You gain a feature from your rogue subclass.

15th Level: Slippery Mind

You become proficient in Wisdom and Charisma saving throws.

16th Level: Feat

You gain the Ability Score Improvement feat or another feat of your choice for which you qualify.

18th Level: Elusive

No attack roll can have advantage against you unless you are Incapacitated.

19th Level: Feat

You gain the Ability Score Improvement feat or another feat of your choice for which you qualify.

20th Level: Stroke of Luck

Once per short or long rest, when you fail a d20 test, you can treat the roll as a 20.

Gambler Rogue

Fortune and luck tend to favor these rogues. They often spend their time in tournaments attempting to twist luck in their favor. Their familiarity with gaming sets allows them to strike at unexpected times.

3rd Level: Adept Competitor

Your prowess at gambling grants you proficiency in a type of gaming set of your choice. Depending on the set that you’re proficient in, you can use a makeshift weapon (which you are proficient in). To get these weapons, simply buy the appropriate gaming set.

Name Damage Properties Group
Dice Set 1d6 bludgeoning Finesse, Light, Loaded, Thrown (range 20) Rogue
Playing Cards 1d6 slashing Finesse, Light, Stacked, Thrown (range 20) Rogue

Properties

  • Loaded. When you roll this weapon’s damage dice, you roll with advantage.

  • Stacked. When you roll this weapon’s damage dice, you can replace one of the die’s results with a different number on that die.

3rd Level: Lucky Number

Choose a number between 1 and 20 to be your lucky number. Whenever you make an ability check, attack roll, or saving throw and roll your lucky number, you gain a d6 inspiration die that lasts 1 minute. Whenever you finish a long rest, you can change your lucky number.

6th Level: Cold Read

You can use your action to study a creature that you can see within 30 ft. of you. For the next minute, you have advantage on all Insight checks and attack rolls against the target- as long as you can see them. You can only gain these benefits against one creature at a time. If you use this feature on a second creature, you gain no advantages against the first creature from this feature.

10th Level: Gambler's Cunning

The following effects are now among your Cunning Strike options:

  • Baneful Strike (Cost: 1d8). The target must make a Wisdom saving throw. On a failed saving throw, they fall under the effects of the Bane spell until the end of their next turn.

  • Fate Stealer (Cost: 3d8). The target must make a Wisdom saving throw. On a failed saving throw, they fall under the effects of the Bestow Curse spell for the next minute.

  • Gambling Strike (Cost: 2d8). When you roll your sneak attack damage on the attack, you can roll 1d20 and add that number to the sneak attack damage.

14th Level: Stacking Fate

Once per long rest, if you or a creature that you can see within 30 ft. of you roll a d20 for an attack roll or ability check, you can use your reaction to change the number that was rolled to a natural 1 or a natural 20. You can do this after they roll, but must do it before the outcome is determined.
