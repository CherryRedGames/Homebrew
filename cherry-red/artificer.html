Proficiencies

Skills: Choose 4 from Arcana, History, Investigation, Medicine, Nature, Perception, Persuasion, Sleight of Hand

2nd Level: Expertise

You gain expertise in two skill proficiencies of your choice.

3rd Level: Artificer Subclass

You gain one artificer subclass of your choice. You gain each of your subclass’s features that are of your artificer level and lower. This class’s description tells you the levels when your subclass provides features.

6th Level: Subclass Feature

You gain a feature from your artificer subclass.

9th Level: Expertise

You gain expertise in two skill proficiencies of your choice.

10th Level: Subclass Feature

You gain a feature from your artificer subclass.

14th Level: Subclass Feature

You gain a feature from your artificer subclass.

Cogwork Mechanic

These artificers channel their magic through the precise, regular movements of cogwork machines. For them, precision and regularity are crucial for everything- especially when it comes to magic. Many of their designs take on a steampunk aesthetic due to the prominence of gears and cogs.

3rd Level: Cogwork Mechanic Spells

You always have certain spells prepared when you reach specific levels in artificer, as shown in the table below. These spells count as artificer spells for you, but they don’t count against the number of artificer spells that you prepare.

Artificer Level Spells
3rd Featherfall, Thunderwave
5th Blur, Hold Person
9th Haste, Slow
13th Compulsion, Freedom of Movement
17th Hold Monster, Temporal Shunt

3rd Level: Clockwork Mechanism

You create a specialized mechanism that helps you focus on your spells through the regular turning of the cogs within. The mechanism can look like a variety of things- as long as it can be easily held or carried and that it is made up of gears and cogs. You can activate your clockwork mechanism as a bonus action a number of times per long rest equal to your Intelligence modifier (minimum 1). When activated, it remains active for one minute (unless you decide to deactivate it early as a bonus action). While it’s active, you gain the following benefits:

  • Once per round, whenever you or a creature within 30 ft. of you, that you can see, rolls a natural 1 or natural 20 on an attack roll, ability check, or saving throw, you can choose to have that creature reroll. They must use the new roll.

  • Your spell attacks deal additional force or lightning damage (your choice) equal to your Intelligence modifier (minimum 1).

3rd Level: Tool Proficiency

You gain proficiency with carpenter’s tools or smith’s tools. If you are already proficient with both, you can choose one other type of artisan’s tools to become proficient in.

6th Level: Chronicity Sense

While your clockwork mechanism is activated, if you see a creature within 60 ft. of you make an attack roll, ability check, or saving throw with advantage or disadvantage, you can use your reaction to cancel the advantage or disadvantage. You can use this feature a number of times per long rest equal to your Intelligence modifier (minimum 1).

10th Level: Heightened Concentration

While your clockwork mechanism is activated, you gain advantage on all concentration checks for your artificer spells and you can concentrate on two artificer spells at once. If you are concentrated on two spells when your clockwork mechanism deactivates, you choose which one you drop concentration on.

14th Level: Cog Jamming

Your channeling of magic through your device has given you a unique connection to time itself. Once per long rest, when you deactivate your clockwork mechanism before its minute of activation is up, you can immediately cast Time Stop without using a spell slot or any material components as an action.

Glassblower

These artificers work with glass. Many of these artificers focus on creating eyewear or other products.

3rd Level: Eyewear

You can spend 1 hour and 15sp worth of materials to create eyewear for a specific creature. A creature wearing this eyewear gains a bonus to Investigation and Perception checks that rely on sight equal to half your proficiency bonus (rounded down). A creature can only benefit from wearing one piece of eyewear at a time.

3rd Level: Glassblower Spells

You always have certain spells prepared when you reach specific levels in artificer, as shown in the table below. These spells count as artificer spells for you, but they don’t count against the number of artificer spells that you prepare.

Artificer Level Spells
3rd Burning Hands, Zephyr Strike
5th Heat Metal, Searing Smite
9th Blinding Smite, Wall of Sand
13th Fire Shield, Greater Invisibility
17th Immolation, Steel Wind Strike

You can cast Heat Metal on glass as well as metal and you can cast the spell as a bonus action. When you cast this spell on a weapon from your Glass Weaponry feature, the weapons deal additional fire damage equal to the fire damage dealt by the spell, but you do not take any fire damage from holding the weapon.

3rd Level: Tool Proficiency

At 3rd level, you gain proficiency with glassblower’s tools. If you are already proficient with glassblower’s tools, you can choose one other type of artisan’s tools to become proficient in. Additionally, you can turn sand into glass with your glassblower’s tools.

6th Level: Extra Attack

You can attack twice, rather than once, whenever you take the attack action.

6th Level: Glass Weaponry

You learn how to create weapons made of glass. As an action, you can expend a spell slot to create one simple melee weapon that deals slashing damage. You can make a weapon this way as long as you have at least one hand free. The glass weapon will appear in your hand. The weapon you create is considered magical and deals additional slashing damage equal to your Intelligence modifier (minimum 1). Whenever you land a weapon attack roll with a glass weapon that you made with this feature, you can immediately use a bonus action to cause the weapon to shatter. The weapon instantly breaks and the creature you attacked takes an additional 3d6 slashing damage. The glass weapon can last for up to 1 minute or until it is shattered.

10th Level: Armor of Glass

You learn the Armor of Agathys spell. However, instead of frost, shrapnel glass coats the outside of your armor and gear. A creature that hits you with a melee attack takes slashing damage from the spell instead of cold.

14th Level: Glass Storm

You're now able to conjure a storm of burning shards of glass. Once per long rest, you can cast Incendiary Cloud at 8th level without using a spell slot. When you cast the spell using this feature, a creature that fails the Dexterity saving throw is also Blinded for the next minute. They can repeat the saving throw at the end of each of their turns to end the Blinded condition early.

Pyrotechnician

A pyrotechnician focuses on the chemical processes behind fireworks, gunpowder, and other processes.

3rd Level: Hand Mortar

You create a hand mortar: a portable firearm with a short barrel to launch special fireworks that you make. Each firework that you make for this feature costs 10sp of materials and takes 15 minutes to produce. As an action, you can launch the firework and make it explode a maximum distance of 120 ft. away. All creatures in a 30 ft. radius of the explosion must make a Dexterity saving throw against your spell save DC. On a failure, creatures take Xd6 fire damage (where X is your Intelligence modifier, minimum 1). On a successful saving throw, they take half the fire damage.

You can apply additional effects to your firework by spending more money in its construction:

  • Radiant. All creatures in a 30 ft. radius of the firework must make a Constitution saving throw against your spell save DC. On a failed saving throw, the creatures suffer Xd4 radiant damage (where X is your Intelligence modifier, minimum 1) and are Blinded until the end of their next turn. On a successful saving throw, they only take half of the radiant damage. You must spend an additional 10sp when constructing your firework to give it this effect.

  • Thundering. All creatures in a 30 ft. radius of the firework must make a Dexterity saving throw against your spell save DC. On a failed saving throw, the creatures suffer Xd4 thunder damage (where X is your Intelligence modifier, minimum 1) and are Deafened until the end of their next turn. On a successful saving throw, they only take half of the thunder damage. You must spend an additional 5sp when constructing your firework to give it this effect.

3rd Level: Pyrotechnician Spells

You always have certain spells prepared when you reach specific levels in artificer, as shown in the table below. These spells count as artificer spells for you, but they don’t count against the number of artificer spells that you prepare.

Artificer Level Spells
3rd Faerie Fire, Guiding Bolt
5th Celebratory Flare, Pyrotechnics
9th Fireball, Fountain of Light
13th Fire Shield, Wall of Fire
17th Cloudkill, Immolation

3rd Level: Tool Proficiency

You gain proficiency with alchemist’s supplies. If you are already proficient with alchemist’s supplies, you can choose one other type of artisan’s tools to become proficient in.

6th Level: Flaming Missiles

You learn the Magic Missile spell if you don’t know it already. It doesn’t count against the number of artificer spells you have prepared. Whenever you cast the spell, each dart deals additional fire damage equal to your Intelligence modifier (minimum 1). Once per long rest, you can cast the spell without expending a spell slot.

10th Level: Showman's Finesse

You can choose to have your allies take no damage from your Pyrotechnician spells or ammunition from your Hand Mortar feature- even if they would be in range.

14th Level: Grand Finale

Once per long rest, before you roll damage, you can choose to double the damage a firework from your Hand Mortar feature would deal.
