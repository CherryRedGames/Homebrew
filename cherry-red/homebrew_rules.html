Combat Rules

Critical Hits with a Weapon

When you land a critical hit with a weapon, you can choose to do one of the following BEFORE you roll damage:

  • Roll your dice as normal, but double the result. For example, attacking with a weapon that has a d6 damage die, you would roll 1d6 and double the number you roll.

  • Roll twice as many dice. For example, attacking with a weapon that has a d6 damage die, you would roll 2d6.

  • Roll as you normally would, and then add the maximum you can roll to the total. For example, attacking with a weapon that has a d6 damage die, you would roll a d6 as you normally would and add the maximum you can roll (6) to the total.

Drawing & Sheathing Weapons

For the purposes of streamlining combat, you can change the weapon(s) you’re holding for a different weapon without using a bonus action or action once per turn.

Holding the Attack Action

If you hold your action to attack a creature, you make all attacks you can as though you’re using your attack action (such as Extra Attack), etc. This does NOT apply to bonus action attacks like the Two-Weapon Fighting feat.

Flanking

When flanking an enemy with an ally that is not Incapacitated, you gain a +2 to attack rolls.

Potions

You can drink a potion as a bonus action. However, if you drink a potion of healing as an action, you don’t roll for healing and instead recover the highest amount total. It is an action to pour a potion down someone else’s throat.

Weapon Scaling

Over time, you become more skilled with your weapon. If you are proficient in a weapon, you can roll an additional damage die at level 5. This rule also applies to unarmed strikes (like the Monk class or Tabaxi race feature).

For example, if you are proficient with simple melee weapons and attack with a handaxe at level 5, you roll 2d6 instead of 1d6. If you land a critical hit, these additional damage dice are doubled as well, so you’d roll 4d6. OR if you are proficient with a musket at level 5, you roll 3d12 instead of 2d12. If you land a critical hit, it becomes 6d12.

Miscellaneous Rules

Exhaustion

For each level of exhaustion a character has (up to 10), that creature’s attack rolls, ability checks, and saving throws are reduced by their level of exhaustion. A creature that reaches 10 levels of exhaustion dies.

Long Rest

Long rests work as normal, except you only restore half of your maximum hit points.

New Monetary System

References to the cost of materials, equipment, or other things located on other pages use an altered monetary system where silver (sp) is used more predominantly thangold (gp). In this system, rewards are typically given out in silver (sp). For a quick conversion between typical 5e prices and this version, change all mentions of sp on these pages to gp in your own games. Below is a table for conversions:

New Spells

Celebratory Flare

Arcane Spell / 2nd Level Transmutation

Casting Time: 1 action

Range: 30 ft.

Components: V, S, M (1 small firework, which the spell uses up)

Duration: Instantaneous

As an action, you send a firework into the air and, as it explodes, gives a creature within the spell’s range inspiration. This inspiration lasts a minute. The explosion above looks like a simple image of your choice.

Crystal Alignment

Primal Spell / 2nd Level Evocation

Casting Time: 1 minute

Range: 5 ft.

Components: S, M (7 gemstones of different colors, each worth at least 15sp each)

Duration: Instantaneous

The 7 gemstones are either placed along the target’s body or the gems levitate in front of the target and shine a magical light on them. The target is healed for 2d6 hit points.

At Higher Levels.

If you cast this spell at 3rd level or higher, you heal an additional 1d6 hit points per spell slot level higher than 2nd level.

Fountain of Light

Divine Spell / 3rd Level Conjuration

Casting Time: 1 action

Range: 60 ft.

Components: V, S, M (phosphorescent moss)

Duration: Instantaneous

A fountain of brilliant light erupts from a point in the spell’s range. Any creature within a 15 ft. radius of the point must make a Constitution saving throw. On a failed saving throw, they take 3d10 radiant damage and are blinded until the end of their next turn. On a successful saving throw, they take half the damage and nothing additional happens.

At Higher Levels.

If you cast this spell using a 4th level spell slot or higher, the radiant damage increases by 1d10 for each additional level above 3rd.

Plasmatic Laser

Arcane Spell / 5th Level Evocation

Casting Time: 1 action

Range: Self (90 ft. line)

Components: V, S

Duration: Instantaneous

A blast of fire and lightning streak out in a line, originating from you and streaking in a line that is 5 ft. wide and 90 ft. long. Any creature caught in the line must make a Dexterity saving throw. On a failed saving throw, the creatures take 5d8 fire damage and 5d8 lightning damage. On a successful saving throw, they take half damage. The spell ignites flammable objects in the area that aren’t being worn or carried.

Spell Alterations

This section covers cahnges to certain spells. If part of a spell is not mentioned, assume that it is the same as normal.

Hex

When you cast this spell using a spell slot of 3rd or 4th level, you can maintain your concentration on the spell for up to 8 hours and the necrotic damage increases by 1d6. When you use a spell slot of 5th level or higher, you can maintain your concentration on the spell for up to 24 hours and the necrotic damage increases by 2d6 instead of 1d6.

Jump

This spell's casting time is now a bonus action.

Additionally, the increase to a creature's jump distance does not subtract from their movement speed.

Mending

This spell's casting time is now an action.

Toll the Dead

An undead crature that would take damage from this spell is considered to be vulnerable to damage taken from this spell.
